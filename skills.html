Methodology & Toolkit

My research practice combines rigorous qualitative analysis with agile design prototyping. Here is how I use these tools to drive insights.

01. Research & Analysis

NVivo (Qualitative Analysis)

How I use it: I use NVivo to code and synthesize massive amounts of interview data. For my PhD, I managed transcripts from 14+ participants, using it to identify the correlation between "Stigma" and "Technology Acceptance".

Used for: Thematic Analysis, Grounded Theory
Qualitative Methods

My Approach: Beyond standard interviews, I specialize in Participatory Action Research (PAR) and Co-Design Workshops. I treat participants not just as subjects, but as co-creators, ensuring the solutions are culturally and socially relevant.

02. Design & Prototyping

Figma

How I use it: I don't just design UI; I use Figma to build High-fidelity Prototypes for usability testing. I also use it to visualize complex frameworks (like the Stigma-Free Framework) to communicate strategies to stakeholders.

Adobe Creative Suite (Ai, Ps)

How I use it: I use Illustrator to draw vector illustrations for academic papers and research diagrams. Photoshop helps me process fieldwork photos to protect participant privacy while maintaining visual impact.

03. Collaboration & Strategy

Miro

How I use it: Miro is my digital whiteboard for remote co-design workshops. I facilitate card-sorting sessions and affinity diagramming with cross-functional teams (NGOs, Developers, Users) to align on product vision.
